General:
This is the lab associated with Physics 233, Electricity and Magnetism. You will have an informational meeting on the first week where you will be assigned your first lab.

Lab Manual: You need to buy the "Experiments in Electricity and Magnetism" lab manual.  It can be purchased for $10 in the Physics Office, Science Annex 107A, from our Secretary (afternoons are best). You will also need a notebook, and you can get one from the office for $2.   Please bring cash.

Don't procrastinate! If you don't have your lab manual by next week you will loose 2 points on your pre-lab.

Grading:
 
Your final letter grade is determined by your professor based on your lab report scores.  The scores are determined by your TA, with guidance (a rubric) from your instructor.   As in previous physics labs, the TA will take off for such things as sloppiness, missing units, and signs of poor understanding in the writing. The score cut-off between A's and B's, B's and C's, etc will depend on the final score distribution produced by the TA, and on departmental policy. Usually about 91% and up is an A.

Course Policies

Attendance  is essential for labs. We will only assist you in finding a make-up time if you had a valid excuse and if there is another section willing to let you sit in. Your instructor, your TA, and the instructor and TA of the other section must all be notified. Your regular TA should always grade your notebook.

Calculators. I encourage you to have a calculator in this lab.
