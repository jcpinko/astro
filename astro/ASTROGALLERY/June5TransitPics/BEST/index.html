Venus Transit 2012

On Tuesday, June 5, ONU Observatory held a viewing of the Transit of Venus from 7:00 to 8:00 PM (EDT).  The viewing was open to the public and about 40 people attended.  (The attendance surely would have been more but the sky was overcast until about 6:30 PM.)  We offered three safe ways to view the Sun, all of which can be seen below.

      Notes about images: 
1) the large dark dot in each image of the Sun is Venus, smaller dots are either sunspots, or marks on the projection screens.
2) The images of the Sun sometimes appear very non-circular.   This is because the beam of light from the telescope was not always perpendicular to the screen, and the line of sight to the camera was also not normal to the screen.
3) The orange color of two pictures is caused by the solar filter in the Questar telescope.  The greenish hue of other images is an artifact of image processing to bring out details.
4) All images taken by Dr. J. Pinkney using a Canon Digital Rebel.
