ONU Astronomy Observatory

Background and Mission
The ONU Observatory has a mission to educate and inspire the students of Ohio Northern University, as well as the surrounding community.  It has been in planning since about 2003 and is finally in its construction phase.  It is scheduled for completion in August, 2010.  The observatory will primarily be used by ONU students in astronomy classes, labs, and in the ONU Astronomy Club.   However, it will also be opened up for public events on a monthly basis.




The ONU Observatory as viewed from the West.  An image of Saturn will be created with a darker colored, raised bricked. It has a roll-off roof design to allow for multiple telescopes to be used simultaneously.  The central observing room will contain three piers with permanently mounted telescopes, reducing the set-up time required to observe.  On the left end is a control room where CCD detectors and telescopes can be operated by computers.   On the right end are the restrooms. The main entrance will be on the North (left) end, although the double doors will be more wheelchair accessible.


   
Location
The ONU Observatory is located on the Northwest side of campus (see map).  Parking is located in the Wander field lot just off of Lincoln Avenue.  This is the same lot where public viewing events have been held in the past.

Public Events

Next Event: Spring Star Party!  Mar 20, 2010  8:30-10:00 pm.


The March 20 event celebrates the vernal equinox - the first day of spring.  It is a special day when the Sun rises due east and sets due west and the length of the day is increasing the fastest.  March 20 also happens to be the day before the opposition of Saturn.  This is when Saturn is closest to Earth and looks the biggest and brightest through a telescope.  Our telescopes will be trained on Saturn as well as Mars.  Even more impressive will be the crescent Moon which will pass through the Pleiades star cluster in Taurus.
 
Dress warmly and arrive at the Wander Field parking lot (see map above) between 8:30 and 10:00! However, don't bother coming if the skies look cloudy.  In case of cancellation, the "rain date" will be Monday, Mar 22 at the same time.
