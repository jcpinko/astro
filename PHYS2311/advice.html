Advice on homework
  • Homework problems will be graded on completeness and correctness (usually only one problem will be checked).
    • Separate solutions by a line and only write 1 column of solutions.
    • Remove any frills from left edge
    • Staple multiple pages together.
    • Late homeworks still receive 50% (5/10 points).
  • The problems are chosen to give the minimum amount of practice with important formulas and concepts. More can be done if you feel that you need practice.
  • Answers to all odd problems are given in the back of the book.
  • Answers to assigned problems, including evens, will be posted on the course web page.
  • Show work. Get in the habit of solving problems in this way:
    • Draw a picture to visualize the situation, if appropriate.
    • Write the knowns and unknowns in the problem and assign them to variables.
    • Write down the formula(s) that you think applies to the problem.
    • Use algebra to solve for the unknown variable.
    • Replace the variables with the numbers and units given.
    • Calculate the answer and verify that the units make sense.
  • If you're having trouble, you can ask me about it during office hours, or ask the physics tutor for help.
