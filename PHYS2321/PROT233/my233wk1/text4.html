Conservation of Charge

  • The net charge of an isolated system remains constant.

    • If an electron is created, then an anti-electron (positron) is also created so the net charge does not change.
      • Called pair-production and can be caused by gamma-rays

  • Charges can move around easily because the electric force is so large compared to gravity

    • Objects do not build up a large amount of charge for very long
      • electrical discharges (“Lightening”)
    • Like charges (both + or both – ) repel
