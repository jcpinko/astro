Electrostatics: E fields and conductors

  • In electrostatics, E fields always point away (perpendicular) from the conductor and E = 0 inside of a conductor.

    • If they did not, electrical currents would exist, F = q E, and charges would move to an equilibrium configuration.

  • Net charge resides on the surface of conductors

  • Charge accumulates at sharp points
