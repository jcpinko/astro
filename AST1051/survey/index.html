Pre-course Survey

Dr. J. Pinkney

December, 2003

These are the sort of questions that I hope you can answer after taking this course. Do not feel bad if you don't know them now.

  1. What causes the seasons?

    1. Periodic changes in cloud cover and precipitation

    2. The tilt of the Earth's spin axis relative to the plane of the Earth's orbit around the Sun

    3. The varying proximity of the Earth to the Sun

    4. The wobbling of the Earth's spin axis.

  2. What are the planets in the solar system, in order of increasing distance from the Sun?

    1. Venus, Mercury, Earth, Jupiter, Mars, Uranus, Saturn, Pluto, Neptune

    2. Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Saturn, Jupiter, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto

    3. Mars, Earth, Mercury, Venus, Saturn, Jupiter, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto

    4. Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto

  3. Which two planets have no Moons?

    1. Jupiter, Neptune (b) Mars, Venus (c) Mercury, Venus (d) Mercury, Pluto

  4. How old is the Solar System?

    1. 4.6 Billion Years (b)1.4 Billion Years (c) 550 Million years (d) Less than 5 Million Years

  5. In general, how do the inner 4 planets differ from the outer planets (except Pluto)?

    1. The inner planets are denser and warmer (b) Only the outer planets have atmospheres (c) All of the inner planets have plate techtonics (continental drift) (d) The outer planets are bluish and the inner planets are all reddish-brown.

  6. What is the difference between astronomy and astrology?

    1. Astrology is always right (b) Astronomy is never wrong (c) Astronomy uses the scientific method to arrive at truth, astrology does not (d) Astronomy is older than astrology

  7. What is the probable cause of the magnetic field around planets?

    1. a solid chunk of magnetized iron in the planet's core (b) a liquid, conductive interior combined with rotation (c) auroral activity (d) entrainment of the Sun's magnetic field

  8. Which is the most massive planet in the solar system?

    1. Jupiter (b) Earth (c) Uranus (d) Neptune

  9. The solar system most likely formed

    1. when two stars collided (b) from a giant, rotating cloud of gas and dust (c) at the same time our Galaxy formed (d) when a passing black hole pulled material off of the Sun

  10. Who discovered Pluto?

    1. William Herschel (b) Carl Sagan (c) Walt Disney (d) Clyde Tombaugh

  11. How is the ``light-year'' defined?

    1. the time it takes for light to travel a year (b) the time it takes for light to travel around the Sun (c) the distance light travels in one year (d) the distance between the Sun and the nearest star

  12. Who proved observationally that Ptolemy's geocentric theory was wrong?

    1. Copernicus (b) Galileo (c) Kepler (d) Newton

  13. About how many planets were known until 1781 (do not include Earth)?

    1. 8 (b) 7 (c) 6 (d) 5

  14. Which planet becomes the brightest in the nighttime sky?

    1. Mercury (b) Venus (c) Jupiter (d) Uranus

  15. Which element is most abundant in the Sun, the universe, and the planet Jupiter?

    1. Iron (b) Lithium (c) Helium (d) Hydrogen
  16. What do YOU hope to learn from this class? That is, are there specific topics that you hope we cover?
